# Superforecasting — Epilogue (parte 2/2)

It probably wouldn’t shatter my image of myself as a reasonably competent superforecaster, but it’d probably make me more cautious and less prone to extreme forecasts….This would probably be a salutary thing, of course: the Brier score gives me feedback
on whether I’m under- or overconfident, and I modify my forecasting behavior in response to it.”

I can’t imagine a better description of the “try, fail, analyze, adjust, try again” cycle—and of the grit to keep at it and keep improving. Bill Flack is perpetual beta. And that, too, is why he is a superforecaster.
